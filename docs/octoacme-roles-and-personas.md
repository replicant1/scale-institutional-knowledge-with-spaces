# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for the user experience and visual design of the product. They translate requirements into user-centered designs and ensure that the end product is intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, user flows, prototypes, and high-fidelity visuals
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into design specifications
- Conduct usability testing and incorporate user feedback
- Review implemented features in demos and sprint reviews to verify design fidelity
- Maintain and evolve the design system and style guidelines

### Goals
- Deliver clear, consistent, and accessible user experiences
- Minimize usability friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure visual and interaction design aligns with product vision

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Participation in sprint planning and demo sessions
- Handoff documentation and annotated design files
- Collaboration with QA/Test Engineers on UI acceptance criteria

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** to understand user needs and align designs with business goals
- Collaborates with **Developers** during implementation to clarify design intent and resolve edge cases
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** to align design milestones with the overall project timeline

---

## QA/Test Engineer

### Role Summary
QA/Test Engineers define and execute the testing strategy to ensure that the product meets quality standards and acceptance criteria. They are advocates for quality throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test plans, test cases, and acceptance criteria verification
- Execute manual and automated tests across functional and non-functional requirements
- Coordinate with Developers on bug triage, reproduction, and fix verification
- Document and track defects, quality risks, and test coverage gaps
- Participate in sprint reviews to validate delivered increments

### Goals
- Ensure all delivered features meet defined acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Reduce defect escape rate and improve overall product reliability
- Promote a quality-first culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Bug reports and defect tracking in project management tools
- Test summary reports shared with Project and Product Managers
- Collaboration with Developers during development for early defect detection
- Participation in sprint ceremonies to provide quality status updates

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works closely with **Developers** to identify, reproduce, and verify fixes for defects
- Aligns with **Product Managers** to validate acceptance criteria and confirm feature completeness
- Reports quality risks and coverage status to **Project Managers** for risk tracking

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions promote security best practices within the team and act as the first line of defense for identifying and mitigating security risks throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Review design documents and implementation for potential security vulnerabilities
- Ensure compliance with organizational security policies and regulatory requirements
- Advise the team on secure coding practices and threat modeling
- Coordinate security reviews and penetration testing activities
- Document and track security risks in the risk register

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Ensure the product meets security and compliance requirements
- Build security awareness and best practices within the team

### Typical Communication
- Security review notes shared with Developers and Product Managers
- Threat model documentation and risk register contributions
- Participation in design reviews and architectural discussions
- Security sign-off before major releases

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Collaborates with **Developers** to identify and remediate security risks during design and implementation
- Works with **Product Managers** to ensure security and compliance requirements are captured as part of the product backlog
- Provides security risk input to **Project Managers** for inclusion in the risk register and release planning

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts support data-driven decision-making by tracking key metrics, preparing reports, and providing insights on product performance and project outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics aligned with project goals
- Prepare and maintain reporting dashboards for stakeholder visibility
- Analyze post-release data to measure feature impact and inform future decisions
- Advise on data collection strategies, A/B test design, and experiment analysis
- Identify trends and anomalies that may affect project priorities or outcomes

### Goals
- Enable the team to make data-informed decisions throughout the project lifecycle
- Provide timely and accurate visibility into product performance
- Support continuous improvement through post-release impact analysis

### Typical Communication
- Weekly or milestone-based reporting to Product and Project Managers
- Dashboard sharing with stakeholders during reviews and briefings
- Collaboration with Product Managers on metric definitions and experiment design
- Post-release analysis reports shared with the broader team

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Aligns with **Product Managers** on success metrics, experiment design, and roadmap prioritization
- Provides performance reporting to **Project Managers** for status updates and decision support
- Works with **Developers** to ensure appropriate data collection and instrumentation are built into features

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent the broader business, user, or sponsor interests in a project. They provide strategic input, review progress, and approve key deliverables at defined milestones.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate business requirements, priorities, and constraints to the project team
- Provide timely feedback on proposed solutions and delivered increments
- Review and approve key project artifacts (e.g., project charter, release plans, major features)
- Escalate blockers or risks that may affect business objectives
- Participate in milestone reviews and retrospectives as appropriate

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers value aligned with business and user needs
- Maintain visibility into project progress and outcomes
- Support timely decision-making to keep the project on track

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based project updates from the Project Manager
- Participation in demo sessions and sprint reviews for key deliverables
- Formal approval workflows for project phases and major releases
- Ad-hoc communication with Product and Project Managers for escalations

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** to align on business priorities and validate that deliverables meet business objectives
- Engages with **Project Managers** to review status, approve decisions, and address escalations
- Provides feedback to **UX/UI Designers** and the broader team during demos and milestone reviews
